Espresso

Espresso is a concentrated coffee beverage that is brewed by forcing hot water under high pressure through a compact amount of finely ground coffee beans. The result is a small, strong shot of coffee with a layer of crema on top. Espresso is the base for many popular coffee drinks, such as cappuccinos and lattes.

Latte

A latte is a coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk. The ratio of espresso to milk can vary, but a common ratio is one-third espresso and two-thirds milk. The milk is steamed to create a frothy texture, and can be flavored with syrups or spices. A latte is typically topped with a layer of foam.

Cappuccino

A cappuccino is a coffee drink made with espresso, steamed milk, and a layer of frothed milk. The ratio of espresso to milk is typically equal, with a small amount of frothed milk added on top. Cappuccinos are often topped with a sprinkle of cocoa powder or cinnamon.
